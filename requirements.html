The History Of Computer Programming


  • 1822

    Charles Babbage came up with the idea of a machine that could calculate complex math problems without error. He called this concept "Difference Engine No. 1"

  • 1843

    A British Woman named Ada Lovelace translates "Notions sur la machine analytique de Charles Babbage" into English ("Elements of Charles Babbageâ€™s Analytical Machine"). She reads the text and is fascinated by Babbage's idea of the computer. She finds out that that a computer can follow a series of instructions to make accurate calculations. She wrote her findings on a piece of paper.

  • 1937

    The Atanasoff-Berry Computer is the first electronic digital computer ever built. It was built to solve simultaneous linear equations.

  • 1947

    Assembly Language is the first digital computer programming language created. It is a low-level programming language that allows programmers to communicate with the hardware of a computer.

  • 1950

    Alan Turing creates a computer that plays chess and also creates the Turing Test. The Turing Test is a test that shows if a machine can display human intelligence.

  • 1983

    Tim Berners-Lee invents a global computer network called the internet. The internet allows computers to connect from all over the world.
